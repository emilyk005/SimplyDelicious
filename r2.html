Roasted Sea Bass & Cream Sauce

Ingredients:

Fish

  • 2 sea bass filets
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1/4 tsp dried basil
  • 1/4 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp garlic powder
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 2 Tbsp Softened Butter

    • Spinach

  • 1 bag washed baby spinach
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • Sauce

  • 1 Tbsp Flour
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 30 mL white wine
  • 60 mL chicken stock
  • 120 mL heavy cream
  • 2 Tbsp grated parmesan
  • Juice and zest of 1/2 a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • Directions:

    1. Preheat oven to 400℉. Butter a baking sheet. Place the fish fillets skin side down on the buttered baking sheet. Combine all the herbs and spices, and sprinkle evenly over fish.
    2. Dot the fish with the softened butter, and set aside while making the sauce.
    3. For the sauce, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour (for gluten free option, I recommend King Arthur GF Flour Blend)
    4. Cook, whisking constantly, for about a minute. Stir in garlic powder, chicken brother, and white wine. Cook until the mixture comes to a boil.
    5. Whisk in cream (for dairy free option, I recommend the Silk Plant Based Heavy Cream), and warm through gently. Whisk in cheese (for dairy free option, I recommend the Follow Your Heart Dairy Free, Vegan, Shredded Parmesan). Add lemon zest and juice. Taste and season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
    6. Place fish in preheated oven and roast for 8 minutes, or until the internal temperature reads 145℉.
    7. While the fish is cooking, make the spinach bed.
    8. Heat a skillet over medium high heat. Add butter and garlic.
    9. Once the butter begins to foam, add the spinach.
    10. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
    11. Serve fish over a bed of spinach, and sauce to your liking.
    12. Enjoy!
